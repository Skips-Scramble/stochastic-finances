# Retirement & Investment Account Rules

| Rule / Feature | Traditional 401(k) | Traditional IRA | Roth 401(k) | Roth IRA | HSA | Individual Brokerage |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Contributions** | Pre-tax (reduce taxable income now) | Pre-tax if deductible; non-deductible also possible | After-tax | After-tax | Pre-tax (or tax-deductible if contributed post-tax) | After-tax (no deduction or tax benefit) |
| **Withdrawals after 59½** | Taxed as ordinary income | Taxed as ordinary income | Tax-free if account held ≥5 yrs | Tax-free if account held ≥5 yrs | Medical: tax-free at any age; Non-medical after 65: taxed as ordinary income (no penalty) | Can withdraw at any time, any age, no penalty; only realized gains are taxed |
| **Maximum withdrawal amount** | No limit (can withdraw entire balance as lump sum) | No limit (can withdraw entire balance as lump sum) | No limit (can withdraw entire balance as lump sum) | No limit (can withdraw entire balance as lump sum) | No limit (can withdraw entire balance) | No limit (can sell and withdraw entire balance at any time) |
| **Withdrawals before 59½** | Tax + 10% penalty (exceptions: disability, Rule of 55, medical, etc.) | Tax + 10% penalty (more exceptions: education, first-home, medical, etc.) | Contributions always after-tax, but withdrawals before 59½ = taxes + 10% penalty (unless exception) | Contributions can be withdrawn anytime tax- & penalty-free; earnings = tax + 10% penalty unless exception | Medical: tax-free at any age; Non-medical before 65: taxed as ordinary income + 20% penalty | No age restriction or penalty; only realized gains are taxed |
| **Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs)** | Yes, starting age 73/75 (must take from each plan) | Yes, starting age 73/75 (can aggregate across IRAs) | ❌ No RMDs starting in 2024 | ❌ No RMDs ever (during your lifetime) | ❌ No RMDs ever | ❌ No RMDs ever |
| **Penalty Exceptions** | Disability, Rule of 55, QDRO, medical >7.5% AGI, SEPP, birth/adoption, disasters | All 401(k) exceptions **plus** higher education & $10k first-time home purchase | Same as traditional 401(k) | Same as traditional IRA | Death or disability (for non-medical before 65) | N/A — no early withdrawal penalties exist |
| **Still working at RMD age** | May delay RMDs if still employed & not 5%+ owner | No delay allowed | May delay under same conditions as traditional 401(k) | Not applicable (no RMDs) | Not applicable (no RMDs) | Not applicable (no RMDs) |
| **Loans allowed?** | ✅ Often, up to $50k or 50% balance | ❌ No | ✅ Often, same as traditional 401(k) | ❌ No | ❌ No | ❌ No (but margin borrowing against holdings is possible) |
| **Income limits on contributions?** | ❌ No (but annual contribution limit applies) | ✅ Yes, deductibility phases out at higher incomes | ❌ No (just contribution limits) | ✅ Yes, direct contributions phased out at higher incomes (backdoor Roth workaround exists) | ❌ No (must be enrolled in HDHP; cannot be on Medicare or claimed as dependent) | ❌ None |
| **Annual contribution limits (2025)** | $23,000 + $7,500 catch-up if 50+ | $7,000 + $1,000 catch-up if 50+ | $23,000 + $7,500 catch-up if 50+ | $7,000 + $1,000 catch-up if 50+ | $4,300 individual / $8,550 family + $1,000 catch-up if 55+ | ❌ None — no cap on how much you can invest |
| **Capital gains taxation** | N/A (all withdrawals taxed as ordinary income) | N/A (all withdrawals taxed as ordinary income) | N/A (qualified withdrawals are tax-free) | N/A (qualified withdrawals are tax-free) | N/A (growth is tax-free inside account) | Short-term (held ≤1 yr): ordinary income rates; Long-term (held >1 yr): 0%, 15%, or 20% |
| **Dividend / interest taxation** | N/A (tax-deferred inside account) | N/A (tax-deferred inside account) | N/A (tax-free inside account) | N/A (tax-free inside account) | N/A (tax-free inside account) | Qualified dividends: 0%/15%/20%; Non-qualified dividends & interest: ordinary income rates |
| **Tax-loss harvesting** | N/A | N/A | N/A | N/A | N/A | ✅ Can offset gains; up to $3,000/yr net losses offset ordinary income; excess carries forward (wash sale rule: no repurchase of substantially identical security within 30 days) |
| **Step-up in basis at death** | N/A (inherited accounts taxed as ordinary income to beneficiary) | N/A (inherited accounts taxed as ordinary income to beneficiary) | N/A (inherited Roth accounts are tax-free to beneficiary) | N/A (inherited Roth accounts are tax-free to beneficiary) | N/A (transferred to beneficiary or becomes part of estate) | ✅ Heirs receive stepped-up cost basis to fair market value at date of death, eliminating unrealized gains |
| **Rollover** | Can roll over to IRA or new employer plan | Can roll over to another IRA or employer plan | Can roll over to Roth IRA or new employer Roth | Can roll over to another Roth IRA | Full balance rolls over year to year (no "use it or lose it" — that is FSAs) | N/A (no rollover concept; transfer between brokerages is possible) |
| **Tax treatment** | Tax now deferred → pay later | Tax now deferred → pay later | Pay tax now → tax-free later | Pay tax now → tax-free later | Triple tax advantage: pre-tax in → tax-free growth → tax-free out (for medical); after 65 non-medical taxed as ordinary income | After-tax in → gains/dividends/interest taxed annually → realized gains taxed at withdrawal |
